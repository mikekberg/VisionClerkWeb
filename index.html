What collectibles and TCGs can I scan?

Vision Clerk is fully functional for English Magic: the Gathering cards, and is expanding to other TCGs. Pokémon is on track to be launched within the month, and our roadmap also includes Yu-Gi-Oh!, One Piece, Flesh and Blood, Lorcana, and more!

Can I use my own camera to scan cards?

Any type of camera can be used to scan! However, we cannot guarantee the quality of the scans, and lower quality images may result in reduced accuracy of results.

I'm looking to catalog my collection. Does Vision Clerk let me do that?

Absolutely! You can scan and view the market prices of cards, as well as save a CSV of the cards that you’ve scanned.

Can Vision Clerk identify card stock variants, promo cards, foils, or card condition?

Vision Clerk’s advanced AI can currently identify card stock variants and is training to identify both promos and foils. You can also easily flag foils and promos within the portal as you scan. As we continue to scan regular and unique card variants, our system will learn to accurately identify those as well! Card conditions are another extremely difficult identification task that Vision Clerk is investigating. We are currently researching the many different grading methodologies and exploring potential solutions. More to come soon! As for card stock, unfortunately our machine has not yet been taught to feel.

What makes Vision Clerk different from other solutions on the market?

Unlike other scanning technologies, which rely on explicit identification techniques that ignore part(s) of the card, Vision Clerk uses cutting edge image recognition that adapts to the complexities of your inventory. This means we're capable of identifying cards with subtle variants, unique sets, and niche art, and can let you know exactly what you’re selling and how much it’s worth.

Will I be able to scan cards in any type of lighting?

Vision Clerks scanning technology is designed to work under your conditions! One of the things that sets Card Clerk apart from the competition is the AI's ability to identify cards under a variety of lighting conditions. However, clearer is always better. Muddled images that would make you squint make it squint as well, so we recommend finding the most well-lit area possible to set up your card scanner. Vision Clerk is always learning, which means that the more exposure our vision system gets to new conditions the more it learns to work under all conditions!
